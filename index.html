Question 2

Take the example of planning a trip to Paris, France.
To find a Paris travel guide, you would begin by searching on a Web 1.0 search engine such as Yahoo! or AltaVista.
Most likely, the results you receive will be static websites that provide basic information about Paris,
such as top tourist attractions, restaurants, and hotels.
The search results and web pages may take some time to sift through.
As an example, consider searching for the same query "Paris travel guide" on a Web 3.0 search engine.
You may receive personalized recommendations for things to do and see in Paris based on your preferences and budget,
such as a virtual assistant or chatbot.
Natural language processing may also be used by the search engine to provide more accurate and detailed information about Paris,
such as local events, cultural activities, and lesser-known attractions.
If you use a Web 3.0 search engine in this scenario,
you may be able to obtain more specific and efficient travel planning information than using a Web 1.0 search engine,
which would provide static information that might not be tailored to your specific needs and preferences.
